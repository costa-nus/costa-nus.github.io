This is the first post from the **CoSTA@NUS** lab. We named the lab after a place
where two systems meet: the cognitive coastline — the line where human minds and
machine intelligence wash up against each other.

## What we're building

The lab works across three pillars:

1. **Cognitive AI** — agents that reason, reflect, and adapt the way people do.
2. **Trustworthy ML** — privacy, robustness, and safety as first-class objectives.
3. **AI for science & health** — frontier methods grounded in real clinical and
   scientific problems.

These aren't separate tracks; they're the same coastline seen from different
angles.

## Why this, why now

The last few years made one thing clear: scaling alone doesn't get us to
systems we can trust to reason carefully under uncertainty. Cognitive science
has a 70-year head start on the questions large models now stumble on —
*memory*, *attention*, *abstraction*, *judgment under noise*. We think the next
decade of AI is built by people fluent in both vocabularies.
